# The Analects of Confucius
## Book 13: Zi Lu (The affairs of the state)

Zi Lu asked about government. The Master said, "Go before the people with your own example, and be laborious in their affairs." He asked again, and the Master said, "Do not beidle."

Zhong Gong being chief minister of the State of Lu, asked about government. The Master said, "First employ the services of your various officers; pardon faults; and give the reward of merit." He replied, "How shall I know who are the men of merit, so that I may give them their reward?" The Master said, "Reward those whom you know. Have you not trustworthy men among your present officers?"

The Master said, "A prince who uses his own personal qualities for government, may compare himself to the north polar star: when once it is fixed, the other stars turn towards it."

Ji Zi Cheng said, "It is easier to govern the country by personal qualifications than by laws." The Master said, "Not so; but when the laws are in the hands of men of high principle, the government is easy; when they are in the hands of men of low principles, it is difficult."

The Master said, "In the government of a country, there are three things of essential importance: the nourishment of the people, the nourishment of the spirits, and the use of the people. Confucius said, 'Which of the three should be first abandoned?' The order should be reversed, for the spirits and then the people. But it is better to abandon the nourishment of the spirits than to abandon the people."

Zeng Xi said, "Good government obtained in a state, one may become rich. But he who, having knowledge, is poor, will not get rich; he who has no knowledge, though rich, will not keep it. Therefore the ruler of a state must first have knowledge."

The Master said, "A country of a thousand chariots cannot be governed without careful attention to business and sincerity. If you attend to business too much, you will lose the substance; if you are earnest, you will not let the people have rest. If you employ the people only in their season, the business will be well done."

The Master said, "In a country, the chief minister should be grave and easy; the officers should be respectful and reverential; the common people should be laborious and harmonious."

The Master said, "The sovereign of a country should not be sparing in expenditure, nor should he love excessive taxation; the one is hurtful, the other is a source of complaint."

The Master said, "A ruler who employs the upright and puts aside the crooked, will be loved by all. A ruler who employs the crooked and puts aside the upright, will be disliked."

Ji Kang asked Confucius about government. Confucius replied, "To govern means to rectify. If you lead on the people with correctness, who will dare not to be correct?" Ji Kang, distressed about the number of thieves in the state, inquired of Confucius how to do away with them. Confucius said, "If you, sir, were not covetous, although you should reward them to do it, they would not steal."

The Master said, "It is all over with me! I have not seen one who loves virtue as he loves beauty."

The Master said, "The way of the superior man is threefold; but I am not equal to it. The superior man is humble in his virtue, respectful in his service to others, and moderate in his desires. Such a man, even if he had the rule of the world, I would not have him."

Fan Chi asked about the way of the superior man. The Master said, "He does not walk in the traces of others, nor take the ways of others for his patterns."

The Master said, "From the man bringing his bundle of dried flesh for my teaching upwards, I have never refused instruction to any one."

Zi Xia said, "If a ruler is personally correct, his government is effective without the issuing of orders. If his personal conduct is not correct, he may issue orders, but they will not be followed."

The Master said, "The governments of Lu and Wei are brothers."

The Master said of Jing, Duke of Wei, that he understood the regulating of the household. When his father was living, he lived in a style suitable to a son; when his father was dead, he lived in a style suitable to a prince.

The Master said, "The affairs of a country may be learned from its ministers."

The Master said, "If a ruler can himself practice the five virtues, what difficulty will he have in government?"

Zi Gong asked about the practice of virtue. The Master said, "The mechanic, who wishes to do his work well, must first sharpen his tools. When you are living in any state, take service with the most worthy among its great officers, and make friends of the most virtuous among its scholars."

The Master said, "With what qualifications may a man be called a scholar?" Zeng Xi answered, "He who has learned the duties of a son and a younger brother, and can in his own conduct maintain them." The Master said, "That is well. But there is another. He who, when he has learned the duties of a son and a younger brother, goes on to serve his ruler and his prince, and in the end preserves his name and his posterity: is he not a scholar?" Zeng Xi said, "What do you say of the man who prefers wealth to righteousness?" The Master said, "He is not a scholar."

### 原文
子路问政。子曰：“先之劳之。”请益，曰：“无倦。”
仲弓为季氏宰，问政。子曰：“先有司，赦小过，举贤才。”曰：“焉知贤才而举之？”曰：“举尔所知。尔所不知，人其舍诸？”
子路曰：“卫君待子而为政，子将奚先？”子曰：“必也正名乎！”子路曰：“有是哉，子之迂也！奚其正？”子曰：“野哉由也！君子于其所不知，盖阙如也。名不正，则言不顺；言不顺，则事不成；事不成，则礼乐不兴；礼乐不兴，则刑罚不中；刑罚不中，则民无所措手足。故君子名之必可言也，言之必可行也。君子于其言，无所苟而已矣。”
樊迟请学稼。子曰：“吾不如老农。”请学为圃。曰：“吾不如老圃。”樊迟出。子曰：“小人哉，樊须也！上好礼，则民莫敢不敬；上好义，则民莫敢不服；上好信，则民莫敢不用情。夫如是，则四方之民襁负其子而至矣，焉用稼？”
子曰：“诵《诗》三百，授之以政，不达；使于四方，不能专对。虽多，亦奚以为？”
子曰：“其身正，不令而行；其身不正，虽令不从。”
子曰：“鲁卫之政，兄弟也。”
子谓卫公子荆：“善居室。始有，曰：‘苟合矣。’少有，曰：‘苟完矣。’富有，曰：‘苟美矣。’”
子适卫，冉有仆。子曰：“庶矣哉！”冉有曰：“既庶矣，又何加焉？”曰：“富之。”曰：“既富矣，又何加焉？”曰：“教之。”
子曰：“苟有用我者，期月而已可也，三年有成。”
子曰：“‘善人为邦百年，亦可以胜残去杀矣。’诚哉是言也！”
子曰：“如有王者，必世而后仁。”
子曰：“苟正其身矣，于从政乎何有？不能正其身，如正人何？”
冉子退朝。子曰：“何晏也？”对曰：“有政。”子曰：“其事也。如有政，虽不吾以，吾其与闻之。”
定公问：“一言而可以兴邦，有诸？”孔子对曰：“言不可以若是其几也。人之言曰：‘为君难，为臣不易。’如知为君之难也，不几乎一言而兴邦乎？”曰：“一言而丧邦，有诸？”孔子对曰：“言不可以若是其几也。人之言曰：‘予无乐乎为君，唯其言而莫予违也。’如其善而莫之违也，不亦善乎？如不善而莫之违也，不几乎一言而丧邦乎？”
叶公问政。子曰：“近者说，远者来。”
子夏为莒父宰，问政。子曰：“无欲速，无见小利。欲速，则不达；见小利，则大事不成。”
叶公语孔子曰：“吾党有直躬者，其父攘羊，而子证之。”孔子曰：“吾党之直者异于是：父为子隐，子为父隐，直在其中矣。”
樊迟问仁。子曰：“居处恭，执事敬，与人忠。虽之夷狄，不可弃也。”
子贡问曰：“何如斯可谓之士矣？”子曰：“行己有耻，使于四方不辱君命，可谓士矣。”曰：“敢问其次。”曰：“宗族称孝焉，乡党称弟焉。”曰：“敢问其次。”曰：“言必信，行必果，硁硁然小人哉！抑亦可以为次矣。”曰：“今之从政者何如？”子曰：“噫！斗筲之人，何足算也？”
子曰：“不得中行而与之，必也狂狷乎！狂者进取，狷者有所不为也。”
子曰：“南人有言曰：‘人而无恒，不可以作巫医。’善夫！”“不恒其德，或承之羞。”子曰：“不占而已矣。”
子曰：“君子和而不同，小人同而不和。”
子贡曰：“乡人皆好之，何如？”子曰：“未可也。”“乡人皆恶之，何如？”子曰：“未可也。不如乡人之善者好之，其不善者恶之。”
子曰：“君子易事而难说也。说之不以道，不说也；及其使人也，器之。小人难事而易说也。说之虽不以道，说也；及其使人也，求备焉。”
子曰：“君子泰而不骄，小人骄而不泰。”
子曰：“刚毅木讷，近仁。”
