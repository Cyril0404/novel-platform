# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第38章

The highest virtue does not display virtue, so it possesses virtue; the lowest virtue never ceases to display virtue, so it lacks virtue. The highest virtue is non‑acting and has no ulterior motive; the lower virtue is acting with an ulterior motive. The highest benevolence acts without ulterior motive; the highest righteousness acts with ulterior motive. The highest courtesy is performed, but if there is no response they raise their arms and fling it away. Thus, when the Way is lost, virtue appears; when virtue is lost, benevolence appears; when benevolence is lost, righteousness appears; when righteousness is lost, courtesy appears. Courtesy is the thinness of loyalty and trust, and the first cause of chaos. Those who pride themselves on foresight are the showy blossoms of the Way, the onset of folly. Therefore the great man dwells in the substantial, not in the superficial; dwells in the real, not in the ornamental. Hence he discards that and takes this.

原文：上德不德，是以有德；下德不失德，是以无德。上德无为而无以为；下德为之而有以为。上仁为之而无以为；上义为之而有以为。上礼为之而莫之应，则攘臂而扔之。故失道而后德，失德而后仁，失仁而后义，失义而后礼。夫礼者，忠信之薄，而乱之首。前识者，道之华，而愚之始。是以大丈夫处其厚，不居其薄；处其实，不居其华。故去彼取此。
