# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第32章

The Tao is forever without a name; plain and small, yet no one under heaven can make it a subject. If lords and kings could hold to it, all things would of themselves become compliant. Heaven and earth unite, sending down sweet dew; the people, without being commanded, are naturally balanced. When the first institutions give rise to names, those names already exist, and one must also know when to stop. Knowing when to stop prevents failure. The Tao’s presence in the world is like that of streams and valleys to the great sea.

原文：道常无名，朴虽小，天下莫能臣也。侯王若能守之，万物将自宾。天地相合，以降甘露，民莫之令而自均。始制有名，名亦既有，夫亦将知止。知止所以不殆。譬道之在天下，犹川谷之于江海。
