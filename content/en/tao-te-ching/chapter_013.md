# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第13章

Favor and disgrace alike cause alarm; the great misfortune is when one regards the body as the self. What does it mean that favor and disgrace alike cause alarm? Favor is lowly; gaining it alarms, losing it alarms, so it is called “favor and disgrace alike cause alarm.” What does it mean that the great misfortune is when one regards the body as the self? The reason we have great misfortune is that we have a self; if we had no self, what misfortune could we have? Therefore, one who values the self for the world can be entrusted with the world; one who loves the self for the world can be entrusted with the world.

原文：宠辱若惊，贵大患若身。何谓宠辱若惊？宠为下，得之若惊，失之若惊，是谓宠辱若惊。何谓贵大患若身？吾所以有大患者，为吾有身，及吾无身，吾有何患？故贵以身为天下，若可寄天下；爱以身为天下，若可托天下。
