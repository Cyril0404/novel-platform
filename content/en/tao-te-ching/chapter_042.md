# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第42章

The Dao begets the one; the one begets the two; the two beget the three; the three beget the myriad things. All things carry yin on their backs and hold yang in their front; their mingled breath forms harmony. What people despise is being orphaned, widowed, childless, yet kings and princes call themselves by these names. Hence a thing may be diminished yet beneficial, or may be increased yet harmful. What others teach, I also teach: the violent and overbearing will not die a natural death. I will take this as the root of teaching.

原文：道生一，一生二，二生三，三生万物。万物负阴而抱阳，冲气以为和。人之所恶，唯孤、寡、不谷，而王公以为称。故物或损之而益，或益之而损。人之所教，我亦教之：强梁者不得其死，吾将以为教父。
