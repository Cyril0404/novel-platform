# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第46章

When the world follows the Tao, the war‑horses are turned back to the fields to spread dung; when the world does not follow the Tao, war‑horses are bred on the outskirts. No misfortune is greater than lack of contentment, and no guilt is greater than the desire to acquire. Hence the contentment that comes from knowing contentment is always sufficient.

原文：天下有道，却走马以粪；天下无道，戎马生于郊。祸莫大于不知足，咎莫大于欲得。故知足之足，常足矣。
