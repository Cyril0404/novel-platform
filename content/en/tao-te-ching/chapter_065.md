# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第65章

The ancient masters of the Dao did not use it to enlighten the people, but to keep them simple. The reason the people are hard to govern is that they have too much knowledge. Therefore, governing a state with knowledge is a disaster for the state; governing a state without knowledge is a blessing. Understanding both of these is also a rule. Constantly knowing this rule is called profound virtue. Profound virtue is deep, far‑reaching, opposite to all things, and then it reaches great harmony.

原文：古之善为道者，非以明民，将以愚之。民之难治，以其智多。故以智治国，国之贼；不以智治国，国之福。知此两者亦稽式。常知稽式，是谓玄德。玄德深矣，远矣，与物反矣，然后乃至大顺。
