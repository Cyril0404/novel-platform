# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第55章

Those whose virtue is full are like newborn infants. Wasps, scorpions, vipers, and snakes do not sting them; fierce beasts do not seize them; birds of prey do not strike them. Their bones are weak and their tendons soft, yet their grasp is firm; without knowledge of the union of male and female, their infant organ rises—this is the utmost of vital essence. They cry all day without becoming hoarse—this is the utmost of harmony. Knowing harmony is called constancy; knowing constancy is called clarity; increasing life is called auspicious; when the heart directs the breath it is called strength. When a thing becomes strong it ages; this is called contrary to the Tao; what is contrary to the Tao soon perishes.

原文：含德之厚，比于赤子。蜂虿虺蛇不螫，猛兽不据，攫鸟不搏。骨弱筋柔而握固，未知牝牡之合而朘作，精之至也。终日号而不嗄，和之至也。知和曰常，知常曰明，益生曰祥，心使气曰强。物壮则老，谓之不道，不道早已。
