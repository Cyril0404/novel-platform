# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第56章

Those who know do not speak; those who speak do not know. Stop up the openings, close the doors; blunt the sharpness, dissolve the tangles; harmonize the light, mingle with the dust—this is called the profound sameness. Hence one cannot be made close, cannot be made distant; cannot be made to profit, cannot be made to suffer; cannot be made noble, cannot be made base. Therefore it is the most honored under heaven.

原文：知者不言，言者不知。塞其兑，闭其门，挫其锐，解其纷，和其光，同其尘，是谓玄同。故不可得而亲，不可得而疏；不可得而利，不可得而害；不可得而贵，不可得而贱。故为天下贵。
