# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第39章

In ancient times those who attained the One: Heaven, by attaining it, became clear; Earth, by attaining it, became tranquil; the spirits, by attaining it, became divine; the valleys, by attaining it, became full; the ten thousand things, by attaining it, came to life; the lords and kings, by attaining it, became the uprightness of the world. If they lost it, Heaven would be rent, Earth would be shattered, the spirits would be extinguished, the valleys would be emptied, the ten thousand things would perish, and the lords and kings would be overthrown. Thus the noble takes the humble as its root, the high takes the low as its foundation. Therefore the lords call themselves “the lonely one,” “the few,” “the unfed.” Is this not because they are rooted in humility? Is it not so? Hence the highest fame is as if there were no fame. Do not desire to be brilliant like jade, nor dull like stone.

原文：昔之得一者：天得一以清，地得一以宁，神得一以灵，谷得一以盈，万物得一以生，侯王得一以为天下贞。其致之：天无以清将恐裂，地无以宁将恐废，神无以灵将恐歇，谷无以盈将恐竭，万物无以生将恐灭，侯王无以贵高将恐蹶。故贵以贱为本，高以下为基。是以侯王自谓孤、寡、不谷。此非以贱为本邪？非乎？故致数舆无舆。不欲琭琭如玉，珞珞如石。
