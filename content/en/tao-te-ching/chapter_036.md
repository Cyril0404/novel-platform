# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第36章

If you want to shrink it, first expand it; if you want to weaken it, first strengthen it; if you want to discard it, first promote it; if you want to take it away, first give it. This is called subtle clarity. Soft weakness conquers hard strength. A fish cannot leave the deep water; a state's sharp weapons must not be shown to the people.

原文：将欲歙之，必固张之；将欲弱之，必固强之；将欲废之，必固兴之；将欲夺之，必固与之。是谓微明。柔弱胜刚强。鱼不可脱于渊，国之利器不可以示人。
