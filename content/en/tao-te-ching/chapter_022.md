# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第22章

Yield, and you become whole;  
be crooked, and you become straight;  
be low, and you become filled;  
be worn, and you become new;  
have little, and you gain;  
have much, and you are confused.  

Thus the sage holds to the One, using it as a model for the world.  

He does not display himself, so he is luminous;  
he does not affirm himself, so he is distinguished;

原文：曲则全，枉则直，洼则盈，敝则新，少则得，多则惑。是以圣人抱一为天下式。不自见故明，不自是故彰，不自伐故有功，不自矜故长。夫唯不争，故天下莫能与之争。古之所谓曲则全者，岂虚言哉！诚全而归之。
