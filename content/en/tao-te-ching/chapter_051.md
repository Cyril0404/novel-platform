# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第51章

The Tao begets them, virtue nourishes them; things give them form, circumstances give them completion. Therefore all things in the world revere the Tao and prize virtue. The reverence for the Tao and the honor of virtue are not commanded but follow naturally. Thus the Tao begets them, virtue nurtures them, makes them grow, rears them, stabilizes them, ripens them, nourishes them, and protects them. It gives life without claiming ownership, acts without depending, and grows without controlling—this is called the profound virtue.

原文：道生之，德畜之，物形之，势成之。是以万物莫不尊道而贵德。道之尊，德之贵，夫莫之命而常自然。故道生之，德畜之，长之育之，亭之毒之，养之覆之。生而不有，为而不恃，长而不宰，是谓玄德。
