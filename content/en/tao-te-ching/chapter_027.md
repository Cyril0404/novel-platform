# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第27章

Excellent walking leaves no tracks, excellent speech leaves no flaw, excellent counting uses no reckoning tools, excellent locking has no bolt and yet cannot be opened, excellent binding has no rope and yet cannot be untied. Therefore the sage is always good at rescuing people, so no one is abandoned; always good at rescuing things, so nothing is discarded. This is called concealed clarity. Hence the good person is the teacher of the not‑good; the not‑good person is the material of the good. He who does not honour the teacher or love the material, though wise, is greatly deluded—this is the essential mystery.

原文：善行无辙迹，善言无瑕谪，善数不用筹策，善闭无关楗而不可开，善结无绳约而不可解。是以圣人常善救人，故无弃人；常善救物，故无弃物。是谓袭明。故善人者，不善人之师；不善人者，善人之资。不贵其师，不爱其资，虽智大迷，是谓要妙。
