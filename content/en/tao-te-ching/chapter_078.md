# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第78章

Nothing under heaven is softer or weaker than water, yet nothing can surpass it in overcoming the hard and strong, because nothing can replace it. The weak conquers the strong, the soft conquers the hard; the world knows this but cannot act upon it. Therefore the sage says, “He who accepts the nation’s disgrace is called the ruler of the state; he who accepts the nation’s misfortune is the king of the world.” True words seem their opposite.

原文：天下莫柔弱于水，而攻坚强者莫之能胜，以其无以易之。弱之胜强，柔之胜刚，天下莫不知，莫能行。是以圣人云：受国之垢，是谓社稷主；受国不祥，是为天下王。正言若反。
