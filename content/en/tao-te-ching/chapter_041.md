# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第41章

When a person of superior virtue hears the Dao, they practice it with diligence; when one of moderate virtue hears the Dao, they are as if holding it and losing it; when one of low virtue hears the Dao, they laugh loudly at it. If it were not laughed at, it would not be the Dao. Therefore it is said: the bright Dao appears dim, the advancing Dao seems to retreat, the even Dao seems rough, the highest virtue is like a valley, the great purity seems disgraced, the extensive virtue seems insufficient, the established virtue seems stolen, the true nature seems changeable, the great square has no corners, the great vessel is formed late, the great sound is rare, the great image has no form, the Dao is hidden and nameless. Only the Dao excels at giving and completing.

原文：上士闻道，勤而行之；中士闻道，若存若亡；下士闻道，大笑之。不笑不足以为道。故建言有之：明道若昧，进道若退，夷道若纇，上德若谷，大白若辱，广德若不足，建德若偷，质真若渝，大方无隅，大器晚成，大音希声，大象无形，道隐无名。夫唯道，善贷且成。
