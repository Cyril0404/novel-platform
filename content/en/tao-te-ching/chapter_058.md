# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第58章

When the government is dull and unobtrusive, the people are simple and honest. When the government is sharp and inspecting, the people are lacking and grasping. Misfortune is what good fortune leans upon; good fortune is what misfortune hides beneath. Who knows where it ends? There is no fixed standard. The straight becomes crooked, the good becomes demonic. People's confusion has lasted a very long time. Therefore the sage is square but not cutting, sharp but not wounding, straight but not overbearing, bright but not dazzling.

原文：其政闷闷，其民淳淳；其政察察，其民缺缺。祸兮福之所倚，福兮祸之所伏。孰知其极？其无正。正复为奇，善复为妖。人之迷，其日固久。是以圣人方而不割，廉而不刿，直而不肆，光而不耀。
