# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第31章

Fine weapons are inauspicious instruments; all things despise them, therefore those who possess the Way do not dwell with them. The noble person, when at rest, values the left; when using military force, values the right. Weapons are inauspicious instruments, not instruments of the noble person; use them only when there is no alternative, and maintain calmness and simplicity as the highest principle. Victory should not be considered beautiful; to consider it beautiful is to delight in killing. Those who delight in killing cannot achieve their aims in the world. Auspicious affairs give priority to the left; inauspicious affairs give priority to the right. The subordinate general occupies the left position; the supreme general occupies the right position—this is to conduct matters according to funeral rites. When many have been killed, one should grieve with sorrow and weeping; even when victorious, conduct matters according to funeral rites.

原文：夫佳兵者，不祥之器，物或恶之，故有道者不处。君子居则贵左，用兵则贵右。兵者不祥之器，非君子之器，不得已而用之，恬淡为上。胜而不美，而美之者，是乐杀人。夫乐杀人者，则不可以得志于天下矣。吉事尚左，凶事尚右。偏将军居左，上将军居右，言以丧礼处之。杀人之众，以哀悲泣之，战胜以丧礼处之。
