# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第63章

Act without acting, do without doing, taste without tasting. Regard the big as small, the many as few; repay resentment with virtue. Seek difficulty in what is easy, seek greatness in what is minute. All difficult matters under heaven must arise from ease; all great matters must arise from smallness. Therefore the sage never strives to be great, and so can become great. He who makes light promises will lack trust; one who regards many things as easy will encounter many difficulties. Thus the sage still treats difficulty as difficulty, and therefore never meets difficulty.

原文：为无为，事无事，味无味。大小多少，报怨以德。图难于其易，为大于其细。天下难事必作于易，天下大事必作于细。是以圣人终不为大，故能成其大。夫轻诺必寡信，多易必多难。是以圣人犹难之，故终无难矣。
