# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第66章

The reason the great rivers and seas can be the sovereigns of all valleys is that they are good at staying low; therefore they become the rulers of the hundred valleys.  
Thus the sage who wishes to be above the people must speak humbly; if he wishes to lead the people, he must put himself behind them.  
Consequently the sage stands above yet the people do not feel burdened, and he stands ahead yet they are not harmed.  
So the world gladly supports him without ever tiring.  
Because he does not contend, no one under heaven can rival him.

原文：江海所以能为百谷王者，以其善下之，故能为百谷王。是以圣人欲上民，必以言下之；欲先民，必以身后之。是以圣人处上而民不重，处前而民不害。是以天下乐推而不厌。以其不争，故天下莫能与之争。
