# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第20章

Give up learning and be free from worries.
How slight the difference between compliance and flattery!
How great the distance between good and evil!
What all people fear, one cannot but fear.
How vast and endless it is!
The crowds are joyful, as if partaking of a great feast,
as if ascending a tower in spring.
I alone remain still, showing no sign,
like an infant not yet capable of laughter,
drifting as if having no home.
The crowds all have abundance,
while I alone seem to lack.
Is this not the heart of a fool, confused indeed?
The vulgar are bright and discerning,
I alone am dim and confused;
the vulgar are sharp and keen,
I alone am dull and silent.
Vast as the sea,
restless as the wind that never ceases.
The crowds all have purposes,
while I alone am stubborn and simple.
I alone differ from others,
valuing nourishment from the source.

原文：绝学无忧。唯之与阿，相去几何？善之与恶，相去若何？人之所畏，不可不畏。荒兮其未央哉！众人熙熙，如享太牢，如春登台。我独泊兮其未兆，如婴儿之未孩，傫傫兮若无所归。众人皆有余，而我独若遗。我愚人之心也哉，沌沌兮！俗人昭昭，我独昏昏；俗人察察，我独闷闷。澹兮其若海，飂兮若无止。众人皆有以，而我独顽似鄙。我独异于人，而贵食母。
