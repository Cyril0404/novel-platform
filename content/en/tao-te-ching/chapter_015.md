# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第15章

The ancient masters who were skilled in the way were subtle, profound, and penetrating, so deep they could not be understood. Because they could not be understood, I can only describe them: cautious as if crossing a river in winter, hesitant as if fearing those on all four sides, dignified as if serving as a guest, fluid as if melting ice, sincere as if uncarved wood, vast as if a valley, murky as if muddy water. Who can be muddy and by stillness gradually become clear? Who can be at rest and through long stillness gradually come to life? Those who hold to this way do not desire fullness. Precisely because they are not full, they can endure without being newly completed.

原文：古之善为士者，微妙玄通，深不可识。夫唯不可识，故强为之容：豫兮若冬涉川，犹兮若畏四邻，俨兮其若客，涣兮其若凌释，敦兮其若朴，旷兮其若谷，混兮其若浊。孰能浊以静之徐清？孰能安以久动之徐生？保此道者不欲盈。夫唯不盈，故能蔽不新成。
