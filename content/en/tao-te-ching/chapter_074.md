# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第74章

The people do not fear death; why then try to frighten them with death? If the people always feared death, and there were those who acted outrageously, I could seize them and put them to death—who would dare? There is always a proper executioner who kills. To kill on behalf of the proper executioner is like taking the place of the great artisan. He who takes the place of the great artisan in cutting wood seldom avoids injuring his own hand.

原文：民不畏死，奈何以死惧之？若使民常畏死，而为奇者，吾得执而杀之，孰敢？常有司杀者杀。夫代司杀者杀，是谓代大匠斫。夫代大匠斫者，希有不伤其手矣。
