# 道德经（Dao De Jing）
## 第53章

If I have but the smallest grain of understanding, walking the great way, I would fear only to go astray. The great way is smooth and easy, yet people prefer the shortcuts. The courts are swept clean, the fields are overgrown with weeds, the granaries are empty. Yet they wear embroidered clothes, carry sharp swords, glut themselves with food and drink, and hoard wealth in excess. This is called bragging of robbery—how contrary to the Dao!

原文：使我介然有知，行于大道，唯施是畏。大道甚夷，而民好径。朝甚除，田甚芜，仓甚虚；服文彩，带利剑，厌饮食，财货有余，是谓盗夸。非道也哉！
