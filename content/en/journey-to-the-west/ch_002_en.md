# 西游记 第2回

## 第2回

第二回 悟彻菩提真妙理 断魔归本合元神

    话表美猴王得了姓名怡然踊跃；对菩提前作礼启谢。那祖师即命大众引悟空出二门外教他洒扫应对进退周旋之节。众仙奉行而出。悟空到门外又拜了大众师兄就于廊庑之间安排寝处。次早与众师兄学言语礼貌、讲经论道习字焚香每日如此。闲时即扫地锄园养花修树寻柴燃火挑水运浆。凡所用之物无一不备。在洞中不觉倏六七年一日祖师登坛高坐唤集诸仙开讲大道。真个是：

    天花乱坠地涌金莲。妙演三乘教精微万法全。

    慢摇麈尾喷珠玉响振雷霆动九天。

    说一会道讲一会禅三家配合本如然。

    开明一字皈诚理指引无生了性玄。

    孙悟空在旁闻听喜得他抓耳挠腮眉花眼笑。忍不住手之舞之足之蹈之。忽被祖师看见叫孙悟空道：“你在班中怎么颠狂跃舞不听我讲？”悟空道：“弟子诚心听讲听到老师父妙音处喜不自胜故不觉作此踊跃之状。望师父恕罪！”祖师道：“你既识妙音我且问你你到洞中多少时了？”悟空道：“弟子本来懵懂不知多少时节。只记得灶下无火常去山后打柴见一山好桃树我在那里吃了七次饱桃矣。”祖师道：“那山唤名烂桃山。你既吃七次想是七年了。你今要从我学些甚么道？”悟空道：“但凭尊祖教诲只是有些道气儿弟子便就学了。”

    祖师道：“‘道’字门中有三百六十傍门傍门皆有正果。不知你学那一门哩？”悟空道：“凭尊师意思。弟子倾心听从。”祖师道：“我教你个‘术’字门中之道如何？”悟空道：“术门之道怎么说？”祖师道：“术字门中乃是些请仙扶鸾问卜揲蓍能知趋吉避凶之理。”悟空道：“似这般可得长生么？”祖师道：“不能！不能！”悟空道：“不学！不学！”

    祖师又道：“教你‘流’字门中之道如何？”悟空又问：“流字门中是甚义理？”祖师道：“流字门中乃是儒家、释家、道家、阴阳家、墨家、医家或看经或念佛并朝真降圣之类。”悟空道：“似这般可得长生么？”祖师道：“若要长生也似‘壁里安柱’。”悟空道：“师父我是个老实人不晓得打市语。怎么谓之‘壁里安柱’？”祖师道：“人家盖房欲图坚固将墙壁之间立一顶柱有日大厦将颓他必朽矣。”悟空道：“据此说也不长久。不学！不学！”

    祖师道：“教你‘静’字门中之道如何？”悟空道：“静字门中是甚正果？”祖师道：“此是休粮守谷清静无为参禅打坐戒语持斋或睡功或立功并入定坐关之类。”悟空道：“这般也能长生么？”祖师道：“也似‘窑头土坯’。”悟空笑道：“师父果有些滴。一行说我不会打市语。怎么谓之‘窑头土坯’？”祖师道：“就如那窑头上造成砖瓦之坯虽已成形尚未经水火煅炼一朝大雨滂沱他必滥矣。”悟空道：“也不长远。不学！不学！”

    祖师道：“教你‘动’字门中之道如何？”悟空道：“动门之道却又怎样？”祖师道：“此是有为有作采阴补阳攀弓踏弩摩脐过气用方炮制烧茅打鼎进红铅炼秋石并服妇乳之类。”悟空道：“似这等也得长生么？”祖师道：“此欲长生亦如‘水中捞月’。”悟空道：“师父又来了！怎么叫做‘水中捞月’？”祖师道：“月在长空水中有影虽然看见只是无捞摸处到底只成空耳。”悟空道：“也不学！不学！”

    祖师闻言咄的一声跳下高台手持戒尺指定悟空道：“你这猢狲这般不学那般不学却待怎么？”走上前将悟空头上打了三下倒背着手走入里面将中门关了撇下大众而去。唬得那一班听讲的人人惊惧皆怨悟空道：“你这泼猴十分无状！师父传你道法如何不学却与师父顶嘴？这番冲撞了他不知几时才出来啊！”此时俱甚抱怨他又鄙贱嫌恶他。悟空一些儿也不恼只是满脸陪笑。原来那猴王已打破盘中之谜暗暗在心所以不与众人争竞只是忍耐无言。祖师打他三下者教他三更时分存心倒背着手走入里面将中门关上者教他从后门进步秘处传他道也。

    当日悟空与众等喜喜欢欢在三星仙洞之前盼望天色急不能到晚。及黄昏时却与众就寝假合眼定息存神。山中又没打更传箭不知时分只自家将鼻孔中出入之气调定。约到子时前后轻轻的起来穿了衣服偷开前门躲离大众走出外抬头观看。正是那：

    月明清露冷八极迥无尘。深树幽禽宿源头水溜汾。

    飞萤光散影过雁字排云。正直三更候应该访道真。你看他从旧路径至后门外只见那门儿半开半掩。悟空喜道：“老师父果然注意与我传道故此开着门也。”即曳步近前侧身进得门里只走到祖师寝榻之下。见祖师蜷局身躯朝里睡着了。悟空不敢惊动即跪在榻前。那祖师不多时觉来舒开两足口中自吟道：

    “难！难！难！道最玄莫把金丹作等闲。

    不遇至人传妙诀空言口困舌头干！”悟空应声叫道：“师父弟子在此跪候多时。”祖师闻得声音是悟空即起披衣盘坐喝道：“这猢狲！你不在前边去睡却来我这后边作甚？”悟空道：“师父昨日坛前对众相允教弟子三更时候从后门里传我道理故此大胆径拜老爷榻下。”祖师听说十分欢喜暗自寻思道：“这厮果然是个天地生成的！不然何就打破我盘中之暗谜也？”悟空道：“此间更无六耳止只弟子一人望师父大舍慈悲传与我长生之道罢永不忘恩！”祖师道：“你今有缘我亦喜说。既识得盘中暗谜你近前来仔细听之当传与你长生之妙道也。”悟空叩头谢了洗耳用心跪于榻下。祖师云：

    “显密圆通真妙诀惜修生命无他说。

    都来总是精气神谨固牢藏休漏泄。

    休漏泄体中藏汝受吾传道自昌。

    口诀记来多有益屏除邪欲得清凉。

    得清凉光皎洁好向丹台赏明月。

    月藏玉兔日藏乌自有龟蛇相盘结。

    相盘结性命坚却能火里种金莲。

    攒簇五行颠倒用功完随作佛和仙。”

    此时说破根源悟空心灵福至切切记了口诀对祖师拜谢深恩即出后门观看。但见东方天色微舒白西路金光大显明。依旧路转到前门轻轻的推开进去坐在原寝之处故将床铺摇响道：“天光了！天光了！起耶！”那大众还正睡哩不知悟空已得了好事。当日起来打混暗暗维持子前午后自己调息。

    却早过了三年祖师复登宝座与众说法。谈的是公案比语论的是外像包皮。忽问：“悟空何在？”悟空近前跪下：“弟子有。”祖师道：“你这一向修些什么道来？”悟空道：“弟子近来法性颇通根源亦渐坚固矣。”祖师道：“你既通法性会得根源已注神体却只是防备着‘三灾利害’。”悟空听说沉吟良久道：“师父之言谬矣。我常闻道高德隆与天同寿水火既济百病不生却怎么有个三灾利害？”祖师道：“此乃非常之道：夺天地之造化侵日月之玄机；丹成之后鬼神难容。虽驻颜益寿但到了五百年后天降雷灾打你须要见性明心预先躲避。躲得过寿与天齐躲不过就此绝命。再五百年后天降火灾烧你。这火不是天火亦不是凡火唤做‘阴火’。自本身涌泉穴下烧起直透泥垣宫五脏成灰四肢皆朽把千年苦行俱为虚幻。再五百年又降风灾吹你。这风不是东南西北风不是和薰金朔风亦不是花柳松竹风唤做‘赑风’。自囟门中吹入六腑过丹田穿九窍骨肉消疏其身自解。所以都要躲过。”悟空闻说毛骨悚然叩头礼拜道：“万老爷垂悯传与躲避三灾之法到底不敢忘恩。”祖师道：“此亦无难只是你比他人不同故传不得。”悟空道：“我也头圆顶天足方履地一般有九窍四肢五脏六腑何以比人不同？”祖师道：“你虽然像人却比人少腮。”原来那猴子孤拐面凹脸尖嘴。悟空伸手一摸笑道：“师父没成算！我虽少腮却比人多这个素袋亦可准折过也。”祖师说：“也罢你要学那一般？有一般天罡数该三十六般变化有一般地煞数该七十二般变化。”悟空道：“弟子愿多里捞摸学一个地煞变化罢。”祖师道：“既如此上前来传与你口诀。”遂附耳低言不知说了些甚么妙法。这猴王也是一窍通时百窍通当时习了口诀自修自炼将七十二般变化都学成了。

    忽一日祖师与众门人在三星洞前戏玩晚景。祖师道：“悟空事成了未曾？”悟空道：“多蒙师父海恩弟子功果完备已能霞举飞升也。”祖师道：“你试飞举我看。”悟空弄本事将身一耸打了个连扯跟头跳离地有五六丈踏云霞去勾有顿饭功夫返复不上三里远近落在面前叉手道：“师父这就是飞举腾云了。”祖师笑道：“这个算不得腾云只算得爬云而已。自古道：‘神仙朝游北海暮苍梧。’似你这半日去不上三里即爬云也还算不得哩！”悟空道：“怎么为‘朝游北海暮苍梧’？”祖师道：“凡腾云之辈早辰起自北海游过东海、西海、南海、复转苍梧苍梧者却是北海零陵之语话也。将四海之外一日都游遍方算得腾云。”悟空道：“这个却难！却难！”祖师道：“世上无难事只怕有心人。”悟空闻得此言叩头礼拜启道：“师父‘为人须为彻’索性舍个大慈悲将此腾云之法一传与我罢决不敢忘恩。”祖师道：“凡诸仙腾云皆跌足而起你却不是这般。我才见你去连扯方才跳上。我今只就你这个势传你个‘筋斗云’罢。”悟空又礼拜恳求祖师却又传个口诀道：“这朵云捻着诀念动真言攒紧了拳对身一抖跳将起来一筋斗就有十万八千里路哩！”大众听说一个个嘻嘻笑道：“悟空造化！若会这个法儿与人家当铺兵送文书递报单不管那里都寻了饭吃！”师徒们天昏各归洞府。这一夜悟空即运神炼法会了筋斗云。逐日家无拘无束自在逍遥此一长生之美。

    一日春归夏至大众都在松树下会讲多时。大众曰：“悟空你是那世修来的缘法？前日师父拊耳低言传与你的躲三灾变化之法可都会么？”悟空笑道：“不瞒诸兄长说一则是师父传授二来也是我昼夜殷勤那几般儿都会了。”大众道：“趁此良时你试演演让我等看看。”悟空闻说抖搜精神卖弄手段道：“众师兄请出个题目。要我变化甚么？”大众道：“就变棵松树罢。”悟空捻着诀念动咒语摇身一变就变做一棵松树。真个是：

    郁郁含烟贯四时凌云直上秀贞姿。

    全无一点妖猴像尽是经霜耐雪枝。大众见了鼓掌呀呀大笑。都道：“好猴儿！好猴儿！”不觉的嚷闹惊动了祖师。祖师急拽杖出门来问道：“是何人在此喧哗？”大众闻呼慌忙检束整衣向前。悟空也现了本相杂在丛中道：“启上尊师我等在此会讲更无外姓喧哗。”祖师怒喝道：“你等大呼小叫全不像个修行的体段！修行的人口开神气散舌动是非生。如何在此嚷笑？”大众道：“不敢瞒师父适才孙悟空演变化耍子。教他变棵松树果然是棵松树弟子们俱称扬喝采故高声惊冒尊师望乞恕罪。”祖师道：“你等起去。”叫：“悟空过来！我问你弄甚么精神变甚么松树？这个工夫可好在人前卖弄？假如你见别人有不要求他？别人见你有必然求你。你若畏祸却要传他；若不传他必然加害：你之性命又不可保。”悟空叩道：“只望师父恕罪！”祖师道：“我也不罪你但只是你去吧。”悟空闻此言满眼堕泪道：“师父教我往那里去？”祖师道：“你从那里来便从那里去就是了。”悟空顿然醒悟道：“我自东胜神洲傲来国花果山水帘洞来的。”祖师道：“你快回去全你性命若在此间断然不可！”悟空领罪“上告尊师我也离家有二十年矣虽是回顾旧日儿孙但念师父厚恩未报不敢去。”祖师道：“那里甚么恩义？你只是不惹祸不牵带我就罢了！”

    悟空见没奈何只得拜辞与众相别。祖师道：“你这去定生不良。凭你怎么惹祸行凶却不许说是我的徒弟。你说出半个字来我就知之把你这猢狲剥皮锉骨将神魂贬在九幽之处教你万劫不得翻身！”悟空道：“决不敢提起师父一字只说是我自家会的便罢。”

    悟空谢了。即抽身捻着诀丢个连扯纵起筋斗云径回东海。那里消一个时辰早看见花果山水帘洞。美猴王自知快乐暗暗的自称道：

    “去时凡骨凡胎重得道身轻体亦轻。

    举世无人肯立志立志修玄玄自明。

    当时过海波难进今日来回甚易行。

    别语叮咛还在耳何期顷刻见东溟。”悟空按下云头直至花果山。找路而走忽听得鹤唳猿啼鹤唳声冲霄汉外猿啼悲切甚伤情。即开口叫道：“孩儿们我来了也！”那崖下石坎边花草中树木里若大若小之猴跳出千千万万把个美猴王围在当中叩头叫道：“大王你好宽心！怎么一去许久？把我们俱闪在这里望你诚如饥渴！近来被一妖魔在此欺虐强要占我们水帘洞府是我等舍死忘生与他争斗。这些时被那厮抢了我们家火捉了许多子侄教我们昼夜无眠看守家业。幸得大王来了！大王若再年载不来我等连山洞尽属他人矣！”悟空闻说心中大怒道：“是甚么妖魔辄敢无状！你且细细说来待我寻他报仇。”众猴叩头：“告上大王那厮自称混世魔王住居在直北下。”悟空道：“此间到他那里有多少路程？”众猴道：“他来时云去时雾或风或雨或雷或电我等不知有多少路。”悟空道：“既如此你们休怕且自顽耍等我寻他去来！”

    好猴王将身一纵跳起去一路筋斗直至北下观看见一座高山真是十分险峻。好山：

    笔峰挺立曲涧深沉。笔峰挺立透空霄曲涧深沉通地户。两崖花木争奇几处松篁斗翠。左边龙熟熟驯驯；右边虎平平伏伏。每见铁牛耕常有金钱种。幽禽□睆声丹凤朝阳立。石磷磷波净净古怪跷蹊真恶狞。世上名山无数多花开花谢繁还众。争如此景永长存八节四时浑不动。诚为三界坎源山滋养五行水脏洞！【□：左“目”右“见”；】美猴王正默看景致只听得有人言语。径自下山寻觅原来那陡崖之前乃是那水脏洞。洞门外有几个小妖跳舞见了悟空就走。悟空道：“休走！借你口中言传我心内事。我乃正南方花果山水帘洞洞主。你家甚么混世鸟魔屡次欺我儿孙我特寻来要与他见个上下！”

    那小妖听说疾忙跑入洞里报道：“大王！祸事了！”魔王道：“有甚祸事？”小妖道：“洞外有猴头称为花果山水帘洞洞主。他说你屡次欺他儿孙特来寻你见个上下哩。”魔王笑道：“我常闻得那些猴精说他有个大王出家修行去想是今番来了。你们见他怎生打扮有甚器械？”小妖道：“他也没甚么器械光着个头穿一领红色衣勒一条黄绦足下踏一对乌靴不僧不俗又不像道士神仙赤手空拳在门外叫哩。”魔王闻说：“取我批挂兵器来！”那小妖即时取出。那魔王穿了甲胄绰刀在手与众妖出得门来即高声叫道：“那个是水帘洞洞主？”悟空急睁睛观看只见那魔王：

    头戴乌金盔映日光明；身挂皂罗袍迎风飘荡。下穿着黑铁甲紧勒皮条；足踏着花褶靴雄如上将。腰广十围身高三丈手执一口刀锋刃多明亮。称为混世魔磊落凶模样。

    猴王喝道：“这泼魔这般眼大看不见老孙！”魔王见了笑道：“你身不满四尺年不过三旬手内又无兵器怎么大胆猖狂要寻我见甚么上下？”悟空骂道：“你这泼魔原来没眼！你量我小要大却也不难。你量我无兵器我两只手勾着天边月哩！你不要怕只吃老孙一拳！”纵一纵跳上去劈脸就打。那魔王伸手架住道：“你这般矬矮我这般高长你要使拳我要使刀使刀就杀了你也吃人笑待我放下刀与你使路拳看。”悟空道：“说得是。好汉子！走来！”那魔王丢开架子便打这悟空钻进去相撞相迎。他两个拳捶脚踢一冲一撞。原来长拳空大短簇坚牢。那魔王被悟空掏短肋撞了裆几下筋节把他打重了。他闪过拿起那板大的钢刀望悟空劈头就砍。悟空急撤身他砍了一个空。悟空见他凶猛即使身外身法拔一把毫毛丢在口中嚼碎望空中喷去叫一声“变！”即变做三二百个小猴周围攒簇。

    原来人得仙体出神变化无方不知。这猴王自从了道之后身上有八万四千毛羽根根能变应物随心。那些小猴眼乖会跳刀来砍不着枪去不能伤。你看他前踊后跃钻上去把魔王围绕抱的抱扯的扯钻裆的钻裆扳脚的扳脚踢打挦毛抠眼睛捻鼻子抬鼓弄直打做一个攒盘。这悟空才去夺得他的刀来分开小猴照顶门一下砍为两段。领众杀进洞中将那大小妖精尽皆剿灭。却把毫毛一抖收上身来。又见那收不上身者却是那魔王在水帘洞中擒去的小猴悟空道：“汝等何为到此？”约有三五十个都含泪道：“我等因大王修仙去后这两年被他争吵把我们都摄将来那不是我们洞中的家火？石盆、石碗都被这厮拿来也。”悟空道：“既是我们的家火你们都搬出外去。”随即洞里放起火来把那水脏洞烧得枯干尽归了一体。对众道：“汝等跟我回去。”众猴道：“大王我们来时只听得耳边风声虚飘飘到于此地更不识路径今怎得回乡？”悟空道：“这是他弄的个术法儿有何难也！我如今一窍通百窍通我也会弄。你们都合了眼休怕！”

    好猴王念声咒语驾阵狂风云头落下。叫：“孩儿们睁眼。”众猴脚屣实地认得是家乡个个欢喜都奔洞门旧路。那在洞众猴都一齐簇拥同入分班齿序礼拜猴王。安排酒果接风贺喜启问降魔救子之事。悟空备细言了一遍众猴称扬不尽道：“大王去到那方不意学得这般手段！”悟空又道：“我当年别汝等随波逐流飘过东洋大海径至南赡部洲学成*人像着此衣穿此履摆摆摇摇云游**年馀更不曾有道；又渡西洋大海到西牛贺洲地界访问多时幸遇一老祖传了我与天同寿的真功果不死长生的**门。”众猴称贺。都道：“万劫难逢也！”悟空又笑道：“小的们又喜我这一门皆有姓氏。”众猴道：“大王何姓？”悟空道：“我今姓孙法名悟空。”众猴闻说鼓掌忻然道：“大王是老孙我们都是二孙、三孙、细孙、小孙、——一家孙、一国孙、一窝孙矣！”都来奉承老孙大盆小碗的椰子酒、葡萄酒、仙花、仙果真个是合家欢乐！咦！贯通一姓身归本只待荣迁仙录箓名。毕竟不知怎生结果居此界终始如何且听下回分解。

**English Translation:**

Chapter 2: Awakening to Patriarch Subodhi's Wonderful Truth, Severing Demons to Unite with Original Spirit

The Handsome Monkey King (Mei Hou Wang) (Sun Wukong) (Mei Hou Wang) was overjoyed to receive his name, bowing respectfully to Patriarch Patriarch Subodhi to express his gratitude. The Patriarch ordered the other disciples to lead Wukong outside the outer gates, where he was taught the proper rites of sweeping, greeting guests, and proper conduct in all situations. The immortals complied, and Wukong bowed to all his elder fellow disciples before arranging his sleeping quarters in the verandas. 

From the next morning onward, he learned language and etiquette with his fellow disciples, discussed scriptures and the Dao (the Way), practiced calligraphy, and burned incense daily. In his free time, he swept the grounds, tended the gardens, planted flowers and trees, gathered firewood, and carried water. Every necessity was provided. Six or seven years passed unnoticed in the cave.

One day, the Patriarch ascended the high altar and gathered all the immortals to lecture on the Great Dao (the Way). It was truly a magnificent sight:
Heavenly flowers rained down, golden lotuses surged from the earth.
He expounded the Three Vehicles of Buddhist teaching, perfect in subtlety and encompassing all Dharma.
Slowly waving his horsetail whisk, his words flowed like pearls and jade, his voice resonant as thunder shaking the Nine Heavens.
He spoke of Dao (the Way) one moment, of Chan Buddhism the next, the three traditions (Confucianism, Buddhism, Daoism) blending perfectly in their fundamental truth.
Each word illuminated the path of sincere devotion, guiding one to transcend birth and death and realize the mystery of one's own nature.

Sun Wukong, listening at the side, was so delighted that he scratched his ears and cheeks, grinning from ear to ear. Unable to contain himself, he began dancing with joy. Suddenly the Patriarch saw him and called out: "Sun Wukong, why are you dancing wildly in the assembly instead of listening to my lecture?"
Wukong replied: "Disciple was listening with full attention, and when I heard your wonderful teachings, I was so overjoyed that I could not help myself from acting this way. I beg Master to forgive me!"
The Patriarch said: "Since you recognize the wonder of the teachings, tell me how long you have been in this cave?"
Wukong said: "Disciple was originally ignorant and does not know how much time has passed. I only remember that when there was no fire in the stove, I often went to the back of the mountain to gather firewood, and there was a mountain with fine peach trees where I have eaten my fill of peaches seven times."
The Patriarch said: "That mountain is called Lantao Shan (Rotten Peach Mountain). If you have eaten peaches seven times, that must be seven years. Now, what kind of Dao (the Way) do you wish to learn from me?"
Wukong said: "Whatever the respected Patriarch teaches, as long as it has the flavor of the Dao (the Way), disciple will learn it."

The Patriarch said: "There are three hundred and sixty secondary schools of the Dao (the Way), all of which can achieve valid results. Which school do you wish to study?"
Wukong said: "I will follow whatever Master wishes. Disciple will obey wholeheartedly."
The Patriarch said: "Shall I teach you the Dao (the Way) of the 'Shu' (Technique) school?"
Wukong said: "What does the Technique school teach?"
The Patriarch said: "The Technique school includes methods for summoning immortals, spirit writing, divination, and fortune-telling, which allow one to know how to seek good fortune and avoid misfortune."
Wukong said: "Can this give me immortality?"
The Patriarch said: "No! No!"
Wukong said: "I won't learn it! I won't learn it!"

The Patriarch then said: "Shall I teach you the Dao (the Way) of the 'Liu' (Flow) school?"
Wukong asked: "What are the principles of the Flow school?"
The Patriarch said: "The Flow school includes Confucianism, Buddhism, Daoism, Yin-Yang school, Mohism, and medicine—either studying scriptures, chanting the Buddha's name, or worshipping sages and immortals."
Wukong said: "Can this give me immortality?"
The Patriarch said: "If you want immortality, this is like 'installing a pillar inside a wall'." *[Idiom: appears solid, but will rot when the building eventually collapses.]*
Wukong said: "Master, I am a simple man and do not understand market idioms. What does 'installing a pillar inside a wall' mean?"
The Patriarch said: "When people build a house and want it to be solid, they place a supporting pillar inside the walls. But one day when the great building collapses, that pillar will surely rot first."
Wukong said: "So that also does not last long. I won't learn it! I won't learn it!"

The Patriarch said: "Shall I teach you the Dao (the Way) of the 'Jing' (Quiescence) school?"
Wukong said: "What kind of valid results come from the Quiescence school?"
The Patriarch said: "This includes abstaining from grain, quiet non-action, Chan meditation, observing vows of silence, fasting, sleep exercises, merit cultivation, deep meditation, and seclusion."
Wukong said: "Can this also give me immortality?"
The Patriarch said: "This is like 'unfired clay bricks at the kiln head'." *[Idiom: formed but not yet hardened by fire, will dissolve in heavy rain.]*
Wukong laughed: "Master really speaks in riddles! You know I don't understand idioms. What does 'unfired clay bricks at the kiln head' mean?"
The Patriarch said: "It is like the unbaked bricks for tiles at the kiln head—though they have taken shape, they have not yet been tempered by water and fire. Once a heavy rain comes, they will turn to mud."
Wukong said: "That also does not last long. I won't learn it! I won't learn it!"

The Patriarch said: "Shall I teach you the Dao (the Way) of the 'Dong' (Action) school?"
Wukong said: "What about the Action school's Dao (the Way)?"
The Patriarch said: "This involves active practices: gathering yin to supplement yang, archery and crossbow practice, energy circulation through the navel, alchemical preparation, firing furnaces and cauldrons, processing cinnabar, refining autumn mineral, and drinking human breast milk, among other things."
Wukong said: "Can this also give me immortality?"
The Patriarch said: "Seeking immortality this way is like 'catching the moon in the water'." *[Idiom: the reflection is visible but cannot be grasped, ultimately empty.]*
Wukong said: "Master, there you go again! What does 'catching the moon in the water' mean?"
The Patriarch said: "The moon is in the sky, and there is a reflection in the water. Though you can see it, there is nothing to grasp—it will ultimately be empty."
Wukong said: "I won't learn this either! I won't learn it!"

Hearing this, the Patriarch gave a sharp shout, jumped down from the high platform, took a ruler in his hand, and pointed at Wukong: "You monkey! You won't learn this, you won't learn that—what do you want?" He stepped forward, struck Wukong three times on the head, then walked inside with his hands behind his back, closed the central gate, and left the assembly behind. All the listening disciples were terrified and scolded Wukong: "You reckless monkey! How dare you talk back to Master when he is trying to teach you the Dao (the Way)? Now that you have offended him, who knows when he will come out again!" At this time, everyone complained about him and looked down on him, but Wukong was not annoyed at all, just smiling broadly.

For the Monkey King (Sun Wukong) had already solved the Patriarch's riddle, keeping it secret in his heart, so he did not argue with the others, only endured silently. The Patriarch striking him three times meant he should be ready at the third watch; walking inside with his hands behind his back and closing the central gate meant he should enter through the back door to receive the secret teachings of the Dao (the Way).

That day, Wukong and the others waited happily in front of the Three Stars Cave (Xueyue Sanxing Dong), anxious for the day to end. At dusk, he went to bed with the others, pretending to close his eyes and calm his spirit. There were no night watches in the mountains to tell the time, so he regulated his breathing to keep track. Around midnight (the Zi hour), he got up quietly, put on his clothes, secretly opened the front gate, slipped away from the others, and went outside to look up at the sky. It was exactly as described:
The moon is bright, the clear dew is cold, the eight extremities are utterly dust-free.
Deep in the trees, the birds roost; at the source, the water flows gently.
Fireflies scatter their light, wild geese write characters across the clouds.
This is the very third watch, the time to seek the true Dao (the Way).

He followed the old path to the back gate, and saw that the door was half-open. Wukong said happily: "Master truly intends to teach me the Dao (the Way), so he left the door open." He stepped softly, slipped inside sideways, and went to the Patriarch's sleeping couch. He saw the Patriarch curled up, sleeping facing inward. Wukong did not dare disturb him, and knelt in front of the couch.

Not long after, the Patriarch woke up, stretched his legs, and chanted to himself:
"Difficult! Difficult! Difficult! The Dao (the Way) is most profound. Do not take the golden elixir for granted.
If you do not meet a true person to transmit the wonderful secrets, you will only waste your breath speaking empty words."

Wukong called out in response: "Master, your disciple has been kneeling here waiting for a long time." When the Patriarch heard Wukong's voice, he immediately got up, put on his robes, sat cross-legged, and shouted: "You monkey! Why are you here in the back instead of sleeping out front?"
Wukong said: "Yesterday before the assembly, Master promised to teach your disciple the Dao (the Way) through the back door at the third watch, so I dared to come boldly to your couch."
The Patriarch was very pleased when he heard this, and thought to himself: "This creature is indeed born of heaven and earth! Otherwise, how could he have solved my secret riddle?"
Wukong said: "There are no other ears here, only your disciple alone. I beg Master, in your great compassion, to teach me the way of immortality. I will never forget your kindness!"
The Patriarch said: "Since you are fated to be here, I am happy to teach you. Now that you have understood my secret riddle, come closer and listen carefully, and I will transmit to you the wonderful Dao (the Way) of immortality." Wukong kowtowed in thanks, listened attentively, and knelt before the couch. The Patriarch said:
"The true wonderful secret of both exoteric and esoteric perfect enlightenment is nothing other than cherishing and cultivating your life force.
All of it is always essence, energy, and spirit—guard them carefully, store them firmly, never let them leak away.
Do not let them leak, keep them stored in your body. When you receive my teachings, the Dao (the Way) will flourish in you.
Memorizing this mantra will bring great benefit: banish evil desires and you will attain cool clarity.
Attain cool clarity, your light will be pure and bright, and you can gaze at the bright moon on the elixir terrace.
The moon hides the Jade Rabbit, the sun hides the Golden Crow; naturally the Tortoise and Snake intertwine.
When they intertwine, your life force will be firm, and you can plant a golden lotus even in fire.
Gather and reverse the Five Elements (Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, Earth); when your practice is complete, you may become a Buddha or immortal as you wish."

Now that the root was revealed, Wukong's mind was open and blessed with insight. He memorized the mantra carefully, thanked the Patriarch deeply, and went out through the back door to look. He saw that the eastern sky was just turning pale, and the western path was shining with golden light. He returned along the old path to the front gate, pushed it open gently, sat down in his original place, and shook the bed, saying: "It's daylight! It's daylight! Get up!" The others were still sleeping, unaware that Wukong had received this great blessing. That day he went about his business as usual, secretly maintaining his practice, regulating his breathing before midnight and after noon.

Three years passed quickly. The Patriarch once again ascended the throne to preach to the assembly. He spoke of koans and allegories, discussed external appearances and superficial forms. Suddenly he asked: "Where is Wukong?" Wukong came forward and knelt: "Disciple is here."
The Patriarch said: "What kind of Dao (the Way) have you been cultivating recently?"
Wukong said: "Disciple's Dharma nature has recently become quite clear, and the root of my practice has gradually grown firm."
The Patriarch said: "Since you have understood the Dharma nature and grasped the root, your spirit body has been established. But you must still beware of the 'Three Calamities'."
When Wukong heard this, he pondered for a long time and said: "Master's words are mistaken. I have always heard that those of high virtue and deep Dao (the Way) live as long as heaven, that water and fire are in balance, and all illnesses are banished—how can there be Three Calamities?"
The Patriarch said: "This is an extraordinary Dao (the Way): it seizes the creative power of heaven and earth, invades the hidden mysteries of sun and moon. Once the elixir is complete, ghosts and gods cannot tolerate you. Though you may preserve your appearance and extend your lifespan, after five hundred years, heaven will send a thunder calamity to strike you. You must see your true nature clearly and avoid it beforehand. If you avoid it, your lifespan equals heaven's; if not, you will die. After another five hundred years, heaven will send a fire calamity to burn you. This fire is not heavenly fire, nor ordinary fire—it is called Yin Fire. It burns from the Yongquan acupoint at the sole of your foot, rising straight through the Niwan Palace in your brain, turning your five internal organs to ash and your four limbs to rot, making a thousand years of bitter practice vanish into illusion. After another five hundred years, heaven will send a wind calamity to blow you. This wind is not the wind from the four directions, not the gentle, hot, golden, or cold winds, nor the wind through flowers, willows, pines, and bamboos—it is called Bi Wind. It blows into your six internal organs through the fontanel gate, passes through the Dantian, and goes through all nine orifices, dissolving your flesh and bones until your body disintegrates of its own accord. So you must avoid all three."

When Wukong heard this, his hair stood on end with terror. He kowtowed and said: "I beg Master, have mercy, and teach me the method to avoid these Three Calamities. I will never forget your kindness to the end."
The Patriarch said: "This is not difficult, but you are different from others, so I cannot teach you."
Wukong said: "I also have a round head supporting heaven, square feet treading earth, the same nine orifices, four limbs, five organs, and six bowels—how am I different from others?"
The Patriarch said: "Though you look like a human, you have less cheek flesh." For the monkey had a high-boned, hollow-cheeked, pointed face. Wukong reached up and touched his face, laughing: "Master miscalculates! Though I have less cheek flesh, I have more of this pouch here, which should count as compensation."
The Patriarch said: "Very well. Which would you like to learn? There is the Tiangang number: 36 transformations, or the Disha number: Seventy-Two Transformations."
Wukong said: "Disciple wishes to take the more abundant one, so I will learn the Seventy-Two Transformations of Disha."
The Patriarch said: "If that is so, come forward and I will transmit the mantra to you." He then whispered in Wukong's ear, and no one knows what wonderful teachings he gave. But the Monkey King (Sun Wukong) was the sort who understands a hundred things once one is explained. He learned the mantra that very day, practiced it on his own, and mastered all Seventy-Two Transformations.

One day, the Patriarch and his disciples were enjoying the evening scenery in front of the Three Stars Cave. The Patriarch said: "Wukong, have you completed your practice?"
Wukong said: "Thanks to Master's great kindness, disciple's practice is complete, and I can now rise into the sky on clouds."
The Patriarch said: "Try flying for me to see." Wukong showed off his skill: he shrugged his body, did a somersault, jumped five or six zhang off the ground, rode the clouds for about the time it takes to eat a meal, and returned, having traveled less than three li. He landed in front of the Patriarch, folded his hands, and said: "Master, this is cloud-sailing and flying."
The Patriarch laughed: "This does not count as riding the clouds, only crawling on them. Since ancient times it is said: 'The immortals travel to the North Sea at dawn and to Cangwu by sunset.' You spent half a day going less than three li—you cannot even be said to crawl on clouds!"
Wukong said: "What does 'travel to the North Sea at dawn and to Cangwu by sunset' mean?"
The Patriarch said: "All who ride the clouds start from the North Sea in the morning, travel through the East Sea, West Sea, South Sea, and return to Cangwu (Cangwu is the ancient name for Lingling in the North Sea region). To be considered riding the clouds, you must travel across all four seas in a single day."
Wukong said: "That is difficult! So difficult!"
The Patriarch said: "There is nothing difficult in the world, only those who lack the heart to try."
When Wukong heard this, he kowtowed and said: "Master, 'if you help someone, help them all the way.' I beseech you, in your great compassion, to teach me this cloud-riding method. I will never dare forget your kindness."
The Patriarch said: "All immortals ride the clouds by stamping their foot, but you do not do that. I just saw you jump up with a somersault. I will teach you a 'Somersault Cloud' based on your particular style." Wukong bowed again and begged, so the Patriarch transmitted the mantra to him: "For this cloud, you twist the incantation, recite the true words, clench your fist, shake your body, jump up, and one somersault will take you 108,000 li!" The other disciples heard this and laughed: "Wukong is lucky! If he masters this technique, he could work as a courier delivering documents and dispatches, and always find enough to eat wherever he goes!" That evening, master and disciples returned to their respective dwellings. That night, Wukong practiced with all his spirit and mastered the Somersault Cloud. Day after day, he was free and unrestrained, enjoying the bliss of immortality.

One day, after spring had given way to summer, all the disciples were gathered under a pine tree discussing the teachings for a long time. The disciples said: "Wukong, what good karma did you cultivate in a past life? The other day Master whispered in your ear and taught you the method to avoid the Three Calamities and the transformations—have you mastered all of them?"
Wukong laughed: "To be honest with you elder brothers, firstly it is Master's teaching, and secondly I have practiced diligently day and night, so yes, I have mastered them all."
The disciples said: "Take this good opportunity to demonstrate for us, let us see." Wukong heard this, roused his spirit, and showed off his skill: "Elder brothers, please give me a subject. What would you have me transform into?"
The disciples said: "Transform into a pine tree." Wukong twisted the incantation, recited the mantra, shook his body, and transformed into a pine tree. Truly:
Luxuriant, wrapped in mist, evergreen through all seasons; soaring to the clouds, upright and chaste in form.
No trace of the demonic monkey remained, only branches that withstand frost and snow.

The disciples applauded and laughed loudly, all saying: "Wonderful monkey! Wonderful monkey!" Their shouting unknowingly alarmed the Patriarch. The Patriarch quickly grabbed his staff and came out the door, asking: "Who is making such a commotion here?" When the disciples heard his call, they hurriedly adjusted their clothes and came forward. Wukong also resumed his original form and stood among the crowd, saying: "Reporting to Master, we are here discussing the teachings, there are no outsiders making noise."
The Patriarch shouted angrily: "You are shouting and yelling—this is no way for cultivators to behave! For those who cultivate the Dao (the Way), when you open your mouth your spiritual energy disperses; when you move your tongue, right and wrong arise. Why are you laughing and shouting here?"
The disciples said: "We dare not hide it from Master. Just now Sun Wukong was demonstrating his transformation technique for fun. We asked him to transform into a pine tree, and he truly became a pine tree. We all praised him and cheered, so we made a loud noise and alarmed Master. We beg for forgiveness."
The Patriarch said: "All of you may go." Then he called: "Wukong, come here! I ask you, what spirit are you showing off, what pine tree are you transforming into? Is this skill something you should show off in front of others? If you see others have something, would you not demand it from them? If others see you have something, they will certainly demand it from you. If you fear disaster, you will have to teach them; if you do not teach them, they will certainly harm you—and your life will be in danger."
Wukong kowtowed: "I only hope Master will forgive me!"
The Patriarch said: "I do not blame you, but you must leave."
When Wukong heard this, tears filled his eyes: "Master, where do you want me to go?"
The Patriarch said: "Where you came from, there you must return."
Wukong suddenly understood: "I came from the Water Curtain Cave on Flower-Fruit Mountain in the Aolai (Proud-Welcome) Kingdom Kingdom of the Eastern Superior Divine Continent."
The Patriarch said: "Return quickly, to save your life. If you stay here, it will absolutely not be allowed."
Wukong accepted his punishment: "Reporting to Master, I have been away from home for twenty years. Though I long to see my children and grandchildren back home, I remember your great kindness has not been repaid, so I dare not leave."
The Patriarch said: "What kindness or righteousness is there? It is enough if you do not cause trouble and drag me into it!"

Wukong saw there was no help for it, so he had to bow and say goodbye to everyone. The Patriarch said: "After you leave, you will certainly create trouble. No matter how much trouble you cause or evil you commit, you are not allowed to say you are my disciple. If you utter even half a word, I will know it, and I will skin you monkey, file your bones to powder, banish your spirit to the Nine Nether Regions, and make you never be reborn for ten thousand kalpas!"
Wukong said: "I will never dare mention Master's name even a single time. I will only say I learned these skills myself."

Wukong thanked him. He then turned, twisted the incantation, did a somersault, and rose on his Somersault Cloud, heading straight back to the Eastern Sea. In less than an hour, he already saw Flower-Fruit Mountain and Water Curtain Cave. The Handsome Monkey King (Mei Hou Wang) (Sun Wukong) was overjoyed, and said to himself quietly:
"When I left, my mortal bones and mortal body were heavy; now that I have attained the Dao (the Way), my body is light and agile.
No one in the world is willing to set their will firmly; those who set their will to cultivate the mysterious Dao (the Way) will naturally understand it.
At that time crossing the ocean waves was difficult; now going back and forth is so easy.
The words of warning are still in my ears; who would have expected I would see the Eastern Sea in an instant?"

Wukong descended his cloud head straight to Flower-Fruit Mountain. As he was looking for the path, he suddenly heard the cry of cranes and the wailing of apes. The crane cries soared beyond the heavens, and the ape cries were sorrowful and heartrending. He called out: "Children, I have returned!" Thousands upon thousands of monkeys, large and small, jumped out from the stone ledges below the cliff, among the flowers and grass, and in the trees. They surrounded the Handsome Monkey King (Mei Hou Wang) (Sun Wukong), kowtowing and shouting: "Great King, you are so carefree! Why did you take so long? You left us here waiting for you, longing as one longs for food and drink! Recently a demon came here bullying us, trying to take our Water Curtain Cave by force. We risked our lives fighting him. All this time he has stolen our household goods and captured many of our children, making us stay awake day and night guarding our property. Fortunately the King has returned! If you had come even a year later, our cave would belong to someone else entirely!"

When Wukong heard this, he was furious: "What demon dares to behave so arrogantly! Tell me the details, and I will go seek revenge on him."
All the monkeys kowtowed: "Reporting to Great King, that fellow calls himself the Demon King of Chaos (Hunshi Mowang), and he lives directly to the north."
Wukong said: "How far is it from here to his place?"
The monkeys said: "He comes on clouds and leaves on mist, sometimes wind, sometimes rain, sometimes thunder, sometimes lightning—we do not know how far the path is."
Wukong said: "If that is so, do not be afraid, just go play. Wait for me to go find him!"

The fine Monkey King (Sun Wukong) leaped up, and with a single somersault traveled all the way north, where he saw an extremely steep mountain. What a fine mountain:
Pen-like peaks stand upright, winding ravines run deep.
The pen-like peaks pierce the empty sky; the winding ravines run deep to the gates of the earth.
On both cliffs, flowers and trees compete in beauty; in several places, pines and bamboo vie in greenness.
The dragon on the left is tamed and gentle; the tiger on the right lies calm and docile.
Often one sees iron oxen plowing, always money seeds being sown.
The secluded bird sings beautifully, the red phoenix stands facing the sun.
Stones are rugged, waves are clear—strange and bizarre, truly fearsome and ferocious.
There are countless famous mountains in the world, with flowers blooming and fading in abundance.
But none compare to this scene, eternal and unchanging through all eight festivals and four seasons.
Truly this is Kanyuan Shan (Source of the Trigram Kan Mountain) of the Three Realms, nurturing the Five Elements (Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, Earth) Water Viscera Cave!

The Handsome Monkey King (Mei Hou Wang) (Sun Wukong) was silently admiring the scenery when he heard voices speaking. He went straight down the mountain to find them, and before the steep cliff he saw the Water Viscera Cave. Several little demons were dancing outside the cave door; when they saw Wukong, they ran away. Wukong said: "Do not run! Let your mouth speak my heart's message. I am the master of Water Curtain Cave on Flower-Fruit Mountain, south of here. What mixed-bird demon of yours has repeatedly bullied my children and grandchildren? I have come specially to settle accounts with him!"

When the little demon heard this, he hurried into the cave to report: "Great King! Disaster!"
The Demon King said: "What disaster?"
The little demon said: "Outside the cave there is a monkey head who calls himself the master of Water Curtain Cave on Flower-Fruit Mountain. He says you have repeatedly bullied his children and grandchildren, and he has come to fight you."
The Demon King laughed: "I often hear those monkey spirits say their great king left to become a monk and cultivate the Dao (the Way). I suppose he has come today. What was he wearing, what weapons did he have?"
The little demon said: "He had no weapons, his head was bare, he wore a red robe, tied with a yellow sash, and wore a pair of black boots. He was neither monk nor layman, not like a Daoist or immortal, shouting outside the door with his bare hands."
The Demon King said: "Bring me my armor and weapons!" The little demon immediately brought them. The Demon King put on his armor, took a broadsword in his hand, and came out the door with all the demons, shouting loudly: "Who is the master of Water Curtain Cave?"
Wukong opened his eyes wide and saw the Demon King:
Wearing a black gold helmet, shining in the sun; wearing a black silk robe, fluttering in the wind.
Below he wears black iron armor, bound tightly with leather straps; on his feet he wears patterned pleated boots, majestic as a great general.
His waist is ten spans around, he is three zhang tall, holding a knife with a bright, sharp blade.
He is called the Demon King of Chaos, his appearance is fierce and formidable.

The Monkey King (Sun Wukong) shouted: "You reckless demon, you are so blind you cannot see Old Sun!"
The Demon King saw him and laughed: "You are less than four chi tall, not yet thirty years old, and you have no weapons in your hand. How dare you be so bold and arrogant to come fight me?"
Wukong cursed: "You stupid demon, you have no eyes! You think I am small, but it is not difficult for me to become large. You think I have no weapons, but my two hands can reach the moon in the sky! Do not be afraid, just take a punch from Old Sun!" He leaped up and punched the Demon King straight in the face.
